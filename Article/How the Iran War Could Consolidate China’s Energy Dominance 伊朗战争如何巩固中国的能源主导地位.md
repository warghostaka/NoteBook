# How the Iran War Could Consolidate China’s Energy Dominance 伊朗战争如何巩固中国的能源主导地位

## Amid global oil and gas disruptions, China stands prepared for the electrostate era. 在全球石油和天然气动荡的背景下，中国已为电国时代做好准备。

By [**Jason Bordoff**](https://foreignpolicy.com/author/jason-bordoff/), a columnist at *Foreign Policy* and the founding director of the Center on Global Energy Policy at Columbia University’s School of International and Public Affairs, and [**Erica Downs**](https://foreignpolicy.com/author/erica-downs/), a senior research scholar at the Center on Global Energy Policy at Columbia University’s School of International and Public Affairs, focusing on Chinese energy markets and geopolitics.
作者：[**Jason Bordoff**](https://foreignpolicy.com/author/jason-bordoff/)，哥伦比亚大学国际与公共事务学院全球能源政策中心创始主任，以及 [**Erica Downs**](https://foreignpolicy.com/author/erica-downs/)，哥伦比亚大学国际与公共事务学院全球能源政策中心高级研究学者，专注于中国能源市场和地缘政治。

March 6, 2026, 5:25 PM

Oil and gas prices have jumped since the United States and Israel struck Iran last weekend, jolting energy markets that had grown complacent about Middle East risk. U.S. gasoline prices are surging, forcing the White House to [weigh](https://www.politico.com/news/2026/03/05/iran-energy-prices-trump-wiles-00813710) how to blunt the political fallout. Europe, just emerging from the shock of Russia’s invasion of Ukraine, faces another potential squeeze, with natural gas prices at their [highest](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-03/european-gas-extends-gains-with-uncertainty-over-qatar-lng-halt) [level](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-04/asian-lng-prices-surge-to-three-year-peak-over-iran-conflict) since 2023.
自上周末美国和以色列袭击伊朗以来，油气价格大幅上涨，震惊了对中东风险已变得麻木的能源市场。美国汽油价格正在飙升，迫使白宫权[衡](https://www.politico.com/news/2026/03/05/iran-energy-prices-trump-wiles-00813710)如何缓和政治影响。刚刚从俄罗斯入侵乌克兰的震惊中走出阴影的欧洲，面临另一波潜在的紧缩，天然气价格达到自 2023 年以来[的最高](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-03/european-gas-extends-gains-with-uncertainty-over-qatar-lng-halt)[水平 ](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-04/asian-lng-prices-surge-to-three-year-peak-over-iran-conflict)。

China, as the world’s [largest](https://www.bairdmaritime.com/shipping/tankers/factbox-global-oil-and-lng-dependence-on-the-middle-east?utm_source=chatgpt.com) [oil](https://www.reuters.com/business/energy/asias-oil-lng-dependence-middle-east-2026-03-02/#:~:text=China is the world's largest crude importer,stockpiles limit the near-term risk of shortages.) and [LNG](https://rbac.com/chinas-lng-surge-surpasses-japan-as-top-lng-importer/#:~:text=In 2023%2C China became the world's largest,imports have been diversified in the future.) importer, would seem the most exposed, and the most likely to be hurt by this shock. Indeed, Beijing has already [ordered](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-05/china-tells-top-refiners-to-suspend-diesel-and-gasoline-exports) refiners to curb fuel exports to protect domestic supplies. But it would be a mistake to assume, [as many observers have](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-02/economists-gauge-hit-from-mideast-war-as-china-seen-among-losers?embedded-checkout=true), that China will be the war’s big loser. Crises often reorder energy geopolitics in unexpected ways. This one may ultimately strengthen, rather than weaken, China’s strategic position.
作为全球[最大的](https://www.bairdmaritime.com/shipping/tankers/factbox-global-oil-and-lng-dependence-on-the-middle-east?utm_source=chatgpt.com)[石油](https://www.reuters.com/business/energy/asias-oil-lng-dependence-middle-east-2026-03-02/#:~:text=China is the world's largest crude importer,stockpiles ​limit the near-term risk of shortages.)和[液化天然气](https://rbac.com/chinas-lng-surge-surpasses-japan-as-top-lng-importer/#:~:text=In 2023%2C China became the world's largest,imports have been diversified in the future.)进口国，中国似乎是最容易受到冲击影响的。事实上，北京已[下令](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-05/china-tells-top-refiners-to-suspend-diesel-and-gasoline-exports)炼油厂限制燃料出口，以保护国内供应。但像[许多观察者那样](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-02/economists-gauge-hit-from-mideast-war-as-china-seen-among-losers?embedded-checkout=true)认为中国将是战争的最大输家，那将是一个错误。危机常常以意想不到的方式重塑能源地缘政治。这次行动最终可能加强而非削弱中国的战略地位。

**Within the first** week of war, oil prices rose more than [25 percent](https://www.barrons.com/articles/crude-oil-prices-brent-wti-iran-today-b640ce7d?gaa_at=eafs&gaa_n=AWEtsqfPyGa3ZbSs2t0udIFeSEoPQKZnlBmfAfswNjtLUOX05k890fYPwBeJhBC4Rds%3D&gaa_ts=69ab4cb8&gaa_sig=Y7-nlplvG61UMF07OMMm1dS25RMAxzxWJxyrh1kbjaIro8Ukn1SpwZ95Cnqc5) and could surge into triple digits if the Strait of Hormuz—through which roughly one-fifth of the world’s oil passes—remains largely closed to tanker traffic. U.S. gasoline prices are now at their highest [level](https://gasprices.aaa.com/) of either of President Donald Trump’s terms and are likely to climb further. And this spike has come even though most major production facilities in the Persian Gulf remain intact. Thankfully, both sides appear worried that direct attacks on energy infrastructure would invite retaliation against their own vital assets.
战争开战的第一周内，油价上涨超过 [25%，](https://www.barrons.com/articles/crude-oil-prices-brent-wti-iran-today-b640ce7d?gaa_at=eafs&gaa_n=AWEtsqfPyGa3ZbSs2t0udIFeSEoPQKZnlBmfAfswNjtLUOX05k890fYPwBeJhBC4Rds%3D&gaa_ts=69ab4cb8&gaa_sig=Y7-nlplvG61UMF07OMMm1dS25RMAxzxWJxyrh1kbjaIro8Ukn1SpwZ95Cnqc5) 如果霍尔木兹海峡——约全球五分之一的石油通过——油轮通行基本封闭，油价可能飙升至三位数。美国汽油价格目前处于特朗普总统任期内的最高[水平 ](https://gasprices.aaa.com/)，且可能进一步上涨。尽管波斯湾大多数主要生产设施依然完好无损，这一激增依然存在。值得庆幸的是，双方似乎都担心直接攻击能源基础设施会招致对自身重要资产的报复。

So far, the disruption has been driven less by physical damage than by logistics and fear. Tankers are avoiding the strait, insurers are raising rates, and producers—particularly Iraq—have begun shutting in output as storage fills without access to export routes.
到目前为止，这种中断主要由后勤和恐惧驱动，而非物理损害。油轮避开了海峡，保险公司提高了保费，生产商——尤其是伊拉克——开始关闭产量，因为储油区充满了无法出口的通道。

Natural gas markets have been hit harder still. After an Iranian drone struck Ras Laffan, the Qatari complex that houses the world’s largest liquefied natural gas (LNG) export facility, Doha suspended operations. Qatar supplies roughly [20 percent](https://www.reuters.com/business/energy/qatars-role-global-gas-market-2026-03-02/) of globally traded LNG, sending shockwaves through already tight markets.
天然气市场受到的冲击更为严重。在伊朗无人机袭击了拉斯拉凡——卡塔尔拥有全球最大液化天然气（LNG）出口设施的综合体后，多哈暂停了运营。卡塔尔供应约 [20%](https://www.reuters.com/business/energy/qatars-role-global-gas-market-2026-03-02/) 的全球液化天然气交易量，这对本已紧张的市场产生了冲击。

China appears highly vulnerable. Roughly [half](https://www.energypolicy.columbia.edu/implications-of-the-conflict-in-the-middle-east-for-chinas-energy-security/) of its crude imports and a third of its LNG imports transit the Strait of Hormuz. With so much at stake, China’s foreign ministry quickly [called](https://www.fmprc.gov.cn/eng/xw/fyrbt/lxjzh/202603/t20260302_11867202.html) for an end to hostilities and for all parties to ensure safe passage through the strait. This is why some analysts have [cast](https://www.cnn.com/world/live-news/iran-israel-us-attack-03-02-26-intl-hnk?post-id=cmm8sbcvs00003b6vdbazvtpg) Beijing as the likely “big loser” of Trump’s strike on Iran.
中国显得极为脆弱。其原油进口约[一半 ](https://www.energypolicy.columbia.edu/implications-of-the-conflict-in-the-middle-east-for-chinas-energy-security/)，液化天然气进口约三分之一通过霍尔木兹海峡。鉴于利害关系重大，中国外交部迅速[呼吁](https://www.fmprc.gov.cn/eng/xw/fyrbt/lxjzh/202603/t20260302_11867202.html)停火，要求各方确保安全通过海峡。这就是为什么一些分析人士将北京[视](https://www.cnn.com/world/live-news/iran-israel-us-attack-03-02-26-intl-hnk?post-id=cmm8sbcvs00003b6vdbazvtpg)为特朗普袭击伊朗的“最大输家”。

Yet over the longer term, there are at least three reasons China may emerge as a surprising beneficiary.
然而，从长远来看，至少有三个原因使中国成为意外受益者。

First, for more than two decades Beijing has pursued an energy security strategy designed precisely for moments like this. At its core is electrification: shifting more of the economy away from direct oil and gas consumption and thereby reducing exposure to volatile oil and gas markets prone to geopolitical disruption.
首先，二十多年来，北京一直致力于为此类时刻设计的能源安全战略。其核心是电气化：将更多经济从直接的石油和天然气消费中转移开来，从而减少对易受地缘政治扰动的石油和天然气市场的暴露。

More than 30 percent of China’s final energy consumption now comes from [electricity](https://ember-energy.org/countries-and-regions/china/#:~:text=Highlights,the rise in electricity demand.), compared with just over [20 percent](https://www.iea.org/reports/electricity-2024/executive-summary) globally. More than half of the cars [sold](https://electrek.co/2025/08/29/electric-vehicles-reach-tipping-point-china-surge-51-market-share/) in China are electric, the result of deliberate policies aimed as much at energy security as emissions reduction. The International Energy Agency estimates China has [avoided](https://www.iea.org/commentaries/oil-demand-for-fuels-in-china-has-reached-a-plateau) 1.2 million barrels per day of oil demand growth since 2019 and now projects Chinese oil demand will peak in [2027](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2025-06-17/china-s-oil-demand-will-peak-earlier-than-expected-iea-says), two years earlier than previously expected.
中国目前超过 30%的最终能源消耗来自[电力 ](https://ember-energy.org/countries-and-regions/china/#:~:text=Highlights,the rise in electricity demand.)，而全球约占 [20%](https://www.iea.org/reports/electricity-2024/executive-summary) 多一点。中国[销售](https://electrek.co/2025/08/29/electric-vehicles-reach-tipping-point-china-surge-51-market-share/)的汽车中，超过一半是电动车，这既是能源安全政策的有意识政策的结果，也是为了减排。国际能源署估计，自 2019 年以来，中国[已避免](https://www.iea.org/commentaries/oil-demand-for-fuels-in-china-has-reached-a-plateau)了每天 120 万桶的石油需求增长，现在预测中国石油需求将在 [2027](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2025-06-17/china-s-oil-demand-will-peak-earlier-than-expected-iea-says) 年达到峰值，比此前预期早两年。

Beijing has also worked to generate as much of its electricity as possible from domestic sources. Coal and renewables [dominate](https://ember-energy.org/data/electricity-data-explorer/?entity=China) the power mix, while nearly all electricity demand [growth](https://ember-energy.org/latest-insights/china-energy-transition-review-2025/) in 2024 was met by clean sources, led by solar and wind. Half of all nuclear reactors under construction worldwide are in China. Although the country imports natural gas, only a [modest](https://ember-energy.org/data/electricity-data-explorer/?entity=China&fuel=total&chart=trend&multi=false&metric=pct_share) share is used for power generation. In the event of prolonged LNG disruptions, China can lean more heavily on domestic sources of energy such as coal to bridge the gap.
北京也努力尽可能多地从国内能源发电。煤炭和可再生能源[主导了](https://ember-energy.org/data/electricity-data-explorer/?entity=China)电力结构，而 2024 年几乎所有电力需求[增长](https://ember-energy.org/latest-insights/china-energy-transition-review-2025/)都由清洁能源满足，其中以太阳能和风能为首。全球一半正在建设的核反应堆位于中国。尽管该国进口天然气，但用于发电的比例仅为[有限 ](https://ember-energy.org/data/electricity-data-explorer/?entity=China&fuel=total&chart=trend&multi=false&metric=pct_share)。如果 LNG 长期中断，中国可以更依赖煤炭等国内能源来弥补缺口。

China would still feel the sting of a global oil shock, of course. But its push to become an electrostate—rather than doubling down on crude production—has reduced its exposure. The United States may be the world’s [largest](https://www.iea.org/countries/united-states) oil producer and a major net exporter, yet because oil is priced globally, American consumers feel the pain at the pump just the same. The most durable hedge against oil shocks is to consume less oil, not merely to produce more.
当然，中国仍会感受到全球石油冲击的刺痛。但其推动成为电态的做法——而不是加倍加大原油生产——反而减少了其暴露。美国可能是世界上[最大的](https://www.iea.org/countries/united-states)石油生产国和主要的净出口国，但由于石油价格在全球范围内，美国消费者同样感受到加油的痛苦。对抗石油冲击最持久的对冲是减少石油消耗，而不仅仅是增加产量。

China has also built buffers. According to Kayrros, it [holds](https://www.energypolicy.columbia.edu/implications-of-the-conflict-in-the-middle-east-for-chinas-energy-security/#:~:text=As of March 2%2C China,will likely flow to China.) roughly 1.4 billion barrels in strategic and commercial storage, which provides China with 120 days of import coverage at the 2025 level. By contrast, the U.S. Strategic Petroleum Reserve is about 40 percent [smaller](https://www.ft.com/content/d546fd5b-4a0c-4b90-8bee-9e85b75458ed) than it was a decade ago. Convinced that the shale revolution had delivered energy independence, Congress sold significant volumes to fund unrelated spending. The Biden administration later released some 200 million barrels after Russia’s invasion of Ukraine to curb gasoline prices, even though Russian exports ultimately proved resilient.
中国也建立了缓冲区。据凯罗斯称，中国在战略和商业储藏中[储存](https://www.energypolicy.columbia.edu/implications-of-the-conflict-in-the-middle-east-for-chinas-energy-security/#:~:text=As of March 2%2C China,will likely flow to China.)约 14 亿桶，为中国 2025 年进口覆盖期达 120 天。相比之下，美国战略石油储备比十年前[减少](https://www.ft.com/content/d546fd5b-4a0c-4b90-8bee-9e85b75458ed)了约 40%。国会坚信页岩革命带来了能源独立，于是大量出售以资助无关支出。拜登政府随后在俄罗斯入侵乌克兰后释放了约 2 亿桶原油以抑制汽油价格，尽管俄罗斯出口最终表现出韧性。

Second, the crisis may shift how other countries weigh energy security trade-offs.
其次，危机可能会改变其他国家权衡能源安全权衡的方式。

In a world where energy is increasingly weaponized, many importers seek to reduce exposure to volatile oil and gas markets by electrifying. Yet electrification introduces a different vulnerability: dependence on China for clean energy technologies.
在能源日益武器化的世界中，许多进口商希望通过电气化减少对油气市场波动的风险。然而，电气化带来了另一种脆弱性：对中国的清洁能源技术依赖。

China’s electrification drive has been paired with a concerted push to dominate clean energy supply chains. It accounts for more than 80 percent of global [solar](https://www.iea.org/reports/solar-pv-global-supply-chains/executive-summary) manufacturing, [wind](https://www.woodmac.com/press-releases/china-oversea-series/) turbines, and [battery](https://www.iea.org/reports/batteries-and-secure-energy-transitions/executive-summary) production capacity and processes the vast majority of critical minerals essential to these technologies. Rapidly expanding grids or deploying large volumes of solar, wind, and storage is exceedingly difficult without deepening reliance on Chinese firms and materials.
中国的电气化推动与主导清洁能源供应链的努力相结合。它[占据全球太阳能](https://www.iea.org/reports/solar-pv-global-supply-chains/executive-summary)制造、[ 风](https://www.woodmac.com/press-releases/china-oversea-series/)力涡轮机和[电池](https://www.iea.org/reports/batteries-and-secure-energy-transitions/executive-summary)生产能力的 80%以上，并加工这些技术所必需的绝大多数关键矿产。快速扩展电网或大规模部署太阳能、风能和储能极为困难，除非加深对中国企业和材料的依赖。

That reality has tempered ambition elsewhere. Europe, for example, aspires to become an electrostate for both climate and security reasons. Yet as our colleagues Anne-Sophie Corbeau and Tatiana Mitrova have [explained](https://nationalinterest.org/blog/energy-world/the-eu-in-a-petrostates-and-electrostates-world), European leaders hesitate to exchange reliance on imported hydrocarbons for dependence on Chinese clean-tech supply chains.
这一现实也抑制了其他领域的雄心。例如，欧洲出于气候和安全原因，渴望成为电国家。然而，正如我们的同事安妮-索菲·科尔博和塔蒂亚娜·米特罗娃[所解释](https://nationalinterest.org/blog/energy-world/the-eu-in-a-petrostates-and-electrostates-world)的，欧洲领导人犹豫着放弃对进口碳氢化合物的依赖，转而依赖中国清洁技术供应链。

This conflict may partially shift that calculation. Dependence on China carries risk. But the reliability of traditional hydrocarbon suppliers appears less certain than it did a few years ago. Europe’s decades-long trust in Russia as a reliable energy supplier is shattered. LNG from the United States has filled much of the gap, yet [concerns](https://www.energypolicy.columbia.edu/reflections-from-munich-2026/) are quietly growing in Europe about the reliability of U.S. LNG exports. Europeans now fear that these could become entangled in domestic politics—whether Washington seeks to use them as a form of economic coercion or restricts exports in response to price spikes at home.
这场冲突可能会在一定程度上改变这一计算。对中国的依赖存在风险。但传统碳氢化合物供应商的可靠性似乎不如几年前那么确定。欧洲对俄罗斯作为可靠能源供应者的数十年信任被打破。美国的 LNG 填补了大部分空白，但欧洲对美国液化天然气出口可靠性的[担忧](https://www.energypolicy.columbia.edu/reflections-from-munich-2026/)正悄然增长。欧洲人现在担心这些可能卷入国内政治——无论华盛顿是试图将其作为经济胁迫手段，还是因国内价格飙升而限制出口。

Now even the Persian Gulf, long an anchor of global supply reliability, looks more vulnerable. For decades Gulf producers were exceptionally reliable. Qatar had never suspended LNG exports until this week. Saudi Arabia restored output with striking speed after the 2019 [attack](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2019-09-15/saudis-race-to-restore-oil-output-after-crippling-aramco-attack) on the Abqaiq facility, demonstrating the durability of its infrastructure. Even now, Saudi Aramco is [rerouting](https://www.marineinsight.com/shipping-news/saudi-arabias-aramco-reroutes-crude-oil-shipments-through-red-sea-port-of-yanbu/?utm_source=rss&utm_medium=rss&utm_campaign=saudi-arabias-aramco-reroutes-crude-oil-shipments-through-red-sea-port-of-yanbu) supplies via pipeline to the Red Sea to meet contractual obligations, underscoring the system’s remarkable flexibility under strain.
如今，连长期以来作为全球供应可靠性锚点的波斯湾，也显得更加脆弱。几十年来，海湾地区的生产商都非常可靠。卡塔尔直到本周才暂停液化天然气出口。沙特在 2019 年阿布盖克设施[袭击](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2019-09-15/saudis-race-to-restore-oil-output-after-crippling-aramco-attack)后迅速恢复产能，展示了其基础设施的耐久性。即使现在，沙特阿美公司仍通过管道将物资[重新导向](https://www.marineinsight.com/shipping-news/saudi-arabias-aramco-reroutes-crude-oil-shipments-through-red-sea-port-of-yanbu/?utm_source=rss&utm_medium=rss&utm_campaign=saudi-arabias-aramco-reroutes-crude-oil-shipments-through-red-sea-port-of-yanbu)红海以满足合同义务，凸显了该系统在压力下极具灵活性。

For years, closure of the Strait of Hormuz was the nightmare scenario that never materialized. Yet if the strait remains largely closed to tanker traffic, importing countries may begin to reassess a risk long treated as theoretical. In that context, reliance on China for electrification components and clean technology may appear less like a strategic liability and more like a manageable trade-off.
多年来，关闭霍尔木兹海峡一直是噩梦般的局面，最终未能实现。然而，如果海峡大体上仍对油轮通行关闭，进口国可能会开始重新评估这一长期被视为理论的风险。在这种背景下，依赖中国提供电气化零部件和清洁技术，可能看起来不像战略负担，而更像是一种可控的权衡。

Third, more broadly, by instigating this crisis without consulting its allies, Washington risks reinforcing the perception that the United States is today’s biggest source of geopolitical volatility. China, by contrast, is seeking to present itself as a steadier commercial partner. The result will be a growing tendency to hedge among traditional U.S. allies. Canada’s decision to ease [restrictions](https://www.pm.gc.ca/en/news/news-releases/2026/01/16/prime-minister-carney-forges-new-strategic-partnership-peoples) on a limited number of Chinese EVs and European leaders’ [visits](https://www.reuters.com/sustainability/boards-policy-regulation/uks-octopus-energy-trade-renewable-energy-china-ties-strengthen-2026-01-30/) to Beijing to deepen clean energy cooperation [reflect](https://www.wilsoncenter.org/article/middle-power-play-why-canada-and-britain-are-hedging-china) this.
第三，更广泛地说，华盛顿在未咨询盟友的情况下挑起这场危机，有可能强化美国是当今最大地缘政治动荡源头的印象。相比之下，中国正寻求将自己塑造成一个更稳定的商业伙伴。结果将是传统美国盟友之间日益倾向于对冲。加拿大决定放宽对有限数量中国电动汽车的[限制 ](https://www.pm.gc.ca/en/news/news-releases/2026/01/16/prime-minister-carney-forges-new-strategic-partnership-peoples)，以及欧洲领导人[访](https://www.reuters.com/sustainability/boards-policy-regulation/uks-octopus-energy-trade-renewable-energy-china-ties-strengthen-2026-01-30/)京以深化清洁能源合作，都[反映了](https://www.wilsoncenter.org/article/middle-power-play-why-canada-and-britain-are-hedging-china)这一点。

China has powerful incentives to cultivate these emerging ties. Clean energy industries—solar, batteries, and EVs—[accounted](https://www.carbonbrief.org/analysis-clean-energy-drove-more-than-a-third-of-chinas-gdp-growth-in-2025/) for more than 11 percent of China’s GDP in 2025 and over a third of its growth. If treated as a standalone economy, this sector would rank among the world’s [largest](https://ember-energy.org/latest-insights/china-energy-transition-review-2025/). Sustaining that expansion requires foreign demand. As energy security concerns intensify, Chinese clean technologies may appear increasingly attractive.
中国有强烈动力去培养这些新兴的联系。清洁能源产业——太阳能、电池和电动汽车——在 2025 年[占](https://www.carbonbrief.org/analysis-clean-energy-drove-more-than-a-third-of-chinas-gdp-growth-in-2025/)中国 GDP 的 11%以上，占其增长的三分之一以上。如果将其视为独立经济体，该行业将跻身全球[最大](https://ember-energy.org/latest-insights/china-energy-transition-review-2025/)经济体之一。维持这种扩张需要外国需求。随着能源安全担忧的加剧，中国的清洁技术可能变得越来越具有吸引力。

**The immediate shock** of this crisis exposes China’s dependence on Middle Eastern oil and gas. But it also underscores how deliberately Beijing has sought to prepare for a world in which energy security is inseparable from geopolitics—by electrifying its economy, securing domestic sources of energy, amassing stockpiles, and dominating clean technology supply chains.
这场危机的直接冲击暴露了中国对中东石油和天然气的依赖。但这也凸显了北京有意为能源安全与地缘政治密不可分的世界做准备——通过电气化经济、保障国内能源、积累库存以及主导清洁技术供应链。

The implications may become obvious soon. As Trump and Chinese President Xi Jinping prepare to meet, Washington is [reportedly](https://www.wsj.com/world/china/u-s-has-a-big-ask-for-china-buy-less-oil-from-russia-more-from-america-3935df4b?gaa_at=eafs&gaa_n=AWEtsqcDpARDVnJXGiI9EvhYjoM8kGohoPt4RFNBphRDY9vJC0KIFn5OU3QRqOklaIg%3D&gaa_ts=69aa4f03&gaa_sig=yJjJj7H_zGDi-prkoWeKHnql6YlpMRt0RHsTsj6T_5SCx3mJ0_U5vUyz9FmKqR6EYxvR10RHUfvsaF9RFW8-0w%3D%3D) considering urging Beijing to redirect oil purchases away from Russia and toward U.S. crude—an effort to wield traditional hydrocarbon leverage at a moment of market stress. Yet China has spent years trying to gradually reduce its structural exposure to precisely such pressure, as well as to the very vulnerabilities that are now rattling markets.
其影响可能很快显现。随着特朗普与中国国家主席习近平准备会面，[ 据报道 ](https://www.wsj.com/world/china/u-s-has-a-big-ask-for-china-buy-less-oil-from-russia-more-from-america-3935df4b?gaa_at=eafs&gaa_n=AWEtsqcDpARDVnJXGiI9EvhYjoM8kGohoPt4RFNBphRDY9vJC0KIFn5OU3QRqOklaIg%3D&gaa_ts=69aa4f03&gaa_sig=yJjJj7H_zGDi-prkoWeKHnql6YlpMRt0RHsTsj6T_5SCx3mJ0_U5vUyz9FmKqR6EYxvR10RHUfvsaF9RFW8-0w%3D%3D)，华盛顿正在考虑敦促北京将石油采购从俄罗斯转向美国原油——这是在市场压力时刻利用传统碳氢化合物杠杆的努力。然而，中国多年来一直试图逐步减少自身对这种压力以及当前市场震动的脆弱性的结构性暴露。

If confidence in global oil and gas trade routes continues to erode while electrification accelerates, this crisis may be remembered as a pivotal moment in the shift toward an electrostate era. And in this new era, China arrives at the bargaining table with a serious and growing advantage.
如果全球石油和天然气贸易路线的信心持续下降，而电气化加速，这场危机或许会被铭记为迈向电国时代的关键时刻。在这个新时代，中国带着严重且日益增长的优势走上谈判桌。
